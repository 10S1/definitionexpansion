A structure ℛ:=⟨𝐺,+,·,0,—,1⟩ is called a ring, if ⟨𝑅,+,0,—⟩ is an Abelian group (called the additive structure), ⟨𝑅,·,1⟩ is a monoid (called the multiplicative structure), and ⟨𝑅,+,·⟩ is a ringoid.
We call +, —, and · the addition, additive inverse, and multiplication operations of ℛ and correspondingly 0 and 1 its zero and one elements respectively. We define 𝑎—𝑏:=𝑎+(—𝑏)𝑎,𝑏 and call it subtraction.
